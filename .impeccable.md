## Design Context

### Users
- Primary users are technical Human Operators, Host AI supervisors, and MCP integrators working locally with terminal-native AI workflows.
- They usually already understand panes, shells, bridges, rooms, and approval flows, but they should not need to think in raw MCP or backend internals to complete routine tasks.
- Core jobs are: launch a Host + Guest pair, supervise delivery, review or approve risky handoffs, inspect runtime health, and recover from stuck or noisy sessions.

### Brand Personality
- Operator-grade
- Local-first
- Approachable under pressure

The interface should feel calm, precise, and trustworthy rather than playful, sales-oriented, or chat-app-like. It should help operators act with confidence during live supervision, review, and incident recovery.

### Aesthetic Direction
- A light-mode control console with mission-control clarity and utilitarian structure.
- Dense information is acceptable, but hierarchy must stay obvious and task-first.
- The GUI should look like high-trust local tooling, not a public SaaS dashboard and not a toy AI playground.
- English and Traditional Chinese should both feel first-class, with technical English preserved where it improves precision.

### Design Principles
- Task first: show the next correct action before showing internals.
- Runtime truth over decorative UI: active state, warnings, and counts must reflect real runtime data.
- Progressive disclosure: keep advanced IDs, diagnostics, and raw state reachable but out of the default path.
- Consistent operator language: use one canonical term per concept across labels, notes, badges, and dialogs.
- Calm under stress: when something is blocked, risky, or waiting for approval, the UI should make that obvious immediately without flooding the operator with noise.
